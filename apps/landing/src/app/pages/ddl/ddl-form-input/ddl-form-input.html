Historical record. Three early visual directions for landing-input + landing-textarea — direction B (sunken card) was picked and shipped, then superseded by the full shipped suite at /ddl/form-lib (input · textarea · checkbox · radio · form-field wired through reactive forms). Kept for the decision trail.

A — Hairline rest, underline focus (rejected)

Transparent background, only a bottom border. On focus the underline thickens to 2px and turns accent. Reads as editorial — the form disappears into the page until you touch it.

B — Sunken card picked, shipped 2026-05-21

Subtle sunken background (ink-1 50%), 1px hairline border, 6px radius. On focus the border turns accent and the background deepens. Reads as a form, clearly — defined affordance, low contrast at rest.

C — Outline + soft accent glow on focus (rejected)

Transparent background, 1.5px hairline border. On focus the border turns accent and a soft 4px glow radiates out. Mid-weight — more designed than A, less boxy than B.

Notes from the decision

  • All variants shipped as the same component with a variant input. The DDL just made the visual difference legible.
  • The component is reactive-form-ready (ControlValueAccessor, supports formControlName) and exposes the same props regardless of variant — picking a default didn't lock anyone out of a per-page override.
  • Variant A pairs best with center-aligned heros (e.g. /uses, /colophon) — it dissolves into the page. Variant B / C handle high-density forms better (e.g. an eventual newsletter, multi-field /contact).
