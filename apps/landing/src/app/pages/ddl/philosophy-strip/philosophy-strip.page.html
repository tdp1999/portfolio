Notes

  • V0 is the current production strip — kept here as a reference for the redundancy + no-break complaint.
  • V1 (cut) wins on rhythm. Story already ends on an italic emphasis ("share a way of working") — a credo line in its own right. Going straight to CTA loses nothing semantic.
  • V2 (squiggle) is the printing-tradition answer to "I need a structural break, but nothing to say." The hand-drawn wave echoes the §6 active-paragraph underline so it reads signature, not generic.
  • V3 (sign-off) treats the whole page like a letter. Best if Story is read as a "from me to you" voice. Risk: datemark needs to feel deliberate, not lorem.
  • V4 (coda) is the most personal — extends §6 visual world. Risk: adds another illustration to maintain across themes.
  • V5a (seal) is a personal mark — strongest "this is mine" signal. Risk: monogram lettering needs custom care to feel intentional, not clipart.
  • V5b (datemark) is the most editorial / book-like — zero decoration, just metadata. Pairs best with V1 minimalism if you want a tiny anchor without imagery.
  • V5c (fin.) is the most literal — "the story ends here". Works only if Story voice is already a bit literary.
  • Recommendation: if undecided, start with V1 (cut) — lowest debt, tightest rhythm. Promote to V2 if the gap feels too abrupt in real layout.
