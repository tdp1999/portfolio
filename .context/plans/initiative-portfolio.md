# Initiative: Portfolio

> A portfolio that helps a Senior FE find better full-time and freelance work — by showing, not telling; by telling stories AI cannot fake; and by being unmistakably the work of one specific human being.

## Why now / why this

- 5 years implementing other people's designs. This is the first thing the author designs (with assistance) end-to-end. Creative ownership matters.
- 3 months invested in this monorepo (console MVP, design bank, context system, patterns docs). The artifacts are real and ready to be shown.
- Current portfolio is unsatisfying and does not represent the author's level. Replacing it is overdue.
- Two life goals are riding on this: higher income, and remote/hybrid work. The portfolio is the surface that makes both reachable.

## North Star

A portfolio that helps a Senior FE find better full-time and freelance work — by showing, not telling; by telling stories AI cannot fake; and by being unmistakably the work of one specific human being.

## Audiences

### Primary — Recruiter / hiring manager (full-time)

- **Job-to-be-done:** decide in 30–90 seconds whether to read further or contact the author.
- **Needs to see fast:** name, role, level, location, primary stack, 1–2 standout artifacts, contact.
- **Reads if interested:** case studies with outcomes, breadth (FE depth + working backend/DDD/system thinking), voice that signals fit.

### Secondary — Freelance client (part-time / 2nd job)

- **Job-to-be-done:** decide whether the author can deliver and is reliable enough to engage.
- **Needs:** outcome-focused case studies, clear contact path, vibe of professionalism without corporate stiffness.
- Overlaps ~70% with recruiter. Build for recruiter first; add a friendly contact section for clients. Do not split the site.

## Differentiator — "the one thing"

The story is mine. AI can match aesthetics; AI cannot fake a real human's specific decisions, trade-offs, opinions, and artifacts. Every section of the site should pass the test: *would this read the same if generated by a stranger with a prompt?* If yes, rewrite it.

## Design Principles — Procida's Twelve Rules

Source: <https://vurt.eu/articles/twelve-rules/>. Treat as constitutional. When any decision is in doubt, run it past these rules. Each sub-epic must reference the rules it is most accountable to.

1. **Answer the damn questions** — every section answers a real question a reader has. No filler sections.
2. **Show, don't just tell** — case studies with artifacts > skill lists. Console MVP is the deepest "show" available; use it.
3. **Demonstrate value** — every project description: before / what changed / outcome. Not "I used X, Y, Z."
4. **Be personal, specific, concrete** — no skill bars, no "advanced JavaScript". "I use Angular daily but reach for plain HTML when…"
5. **Show the parts, not the whole** — depth on a few things beats encyclopedic coverage.
6. **Use perspectives** — when discussing open topics, name the lens (personal / team / industry).
7. **Tell stories** — narrative beats abstract claims. Console case study is a story, not a feature list.
8. **Be proud** — own it without hedging or "just a side project lol". This is the author's work.
9. **Admit vulnerability** — what the author does not know, or has not done, can be said plainly.
10. **Confront weaknesses** — addressed in About / FAQ tone, not hidden.
11. **Do your research** — applied at the *consumer* of the portfolio: the site should feel like the author has thought about who is reading.
12. **Be a human being** — no AI-generic copy. If a sentence could appear on 1000 other portfolios, rewrite it.

## V1 Scope — what "ship" means

**V1 must:** be a credible visual CV that the author would send to a hiring manager without a disclaimer. Specifically:

- Hero with name, role, level, location, one-sentence positioning.
- About section with voice — who the author is as an engineer and a person.
- At least one deep case study (Console MVP) showing process, decisions, artifacts.
- A short list of additional projects with outcome framing.
- Skills framed by Rule 4 (specific, situated), not bars.
- Contact section friendly to both recruiters and freelance clients.
- English-language. VN parking-lot must be cheap to add later (i18n-ready structure, but not implemented in v1).
- Lighthouse ≥ 95 across the four scores.
- OG image, basic SEO, JSON-LD `Person`.
- No scroll-triggered content reveal. Content is readable immediately on every section.

**V1 must not:**

- Personal-expression / blog / hobby content. That belongs to a different site.
- Maintenance commitments. No "weekly post" promises.
- AI-generic phrasing.
- Skill bars or generic skill matrices.
- Scroll-jacking, mandatory animations to read content, or any "wait for animation" UX.
- A standalone product landing of the console app. Console is a case study, not the centerpiece.

## Voice & language

- **Voice:** conversational, the author's natural register. Not corporate. Not ironic-self-deprecating. Proud without bragging (Rule 8).
- **Language:** English primary in v1. Vietnamese as a v2 add-on. Architecture must support i18n cheaply (route or toggle), but no VN content ships in v1.

## Lifecycle

- Ship-and-forget by default. No commitment to update.
- Author may add projects or case studies opportunistically; the site must not depend on a cadence to feel alive.
- Avoid CMS unless a sub-epic proves a real need. Hand-curated content is preferred for v1.

## Risks

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| -- | ---- | ---------- | ------ | ---------- |
| R1 | Scope creep / perfectionism — no deadline, subjective success criteria | High | High | V1 scope above is the contract. Anything outside goes to parking lot. Re-read this section weekly. |
| R2 | Big-tech roles list "SE" not "FE" | Medium | Medium | Lean into breadth: console case study shows backend (NestJS DDD, Prisma) + FE depth + system design. The author is full-stack with FE depth, and the site must say so. |
| R3 | Voice loss when writing in English | Medium | Medium | Draft directly in English in the author's natural voice. No machine translation from VN drafts. Read aloud test. |
| R4 | "3 friends approve" is a subjective oracle | Low | Low | Add objective floor: Lighthouse ≥ 95, ≥ 1 inbound contact in 90 days post-launch. |
| R5 | Console case study balloons into the site | Medium | Medium | Console is the deepest case study, not the centerpiece. Allocated weight in IA: ~30–40% of case-study real estate, not the front door. |

## Success metrics

**Primary (subjective):**

- The author looks at the shipped site and feels proud (Rule 8). Specifically: would forward to a hiring manager today, no disclaimer.
- 3 trusted reviewers (better visual taste than the author) agree it is good.

**Secondary (objective floor):**

- Lighthouse ≥ 95 across performance, accessibility, best-practices, SEO.
- ≥ 1 inbound contact via the portfolio within 90 days of launch.
- 0 AI-generic sentences identifiable on a re-read after 1 week.

## Anti-goals (locked)

- Not a personal-expression / blog / hobby site.
- Not a product landing of the console app.
- Not an AI-generated-looking site, regardless of what tool helped build it.
- Not a maintenance burden.
- Not a scroll-jacking experience.

## Parking lot (v2+)

- Vietnamese localization.
- Personal-expression site (separate domain / project).
- Blog or articles cadence.
- CMS / dynamic content management.
- Live console demo embedded inline (if the visual signature work in E4 calls for it, may be promoted into v1).
